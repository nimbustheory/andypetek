# Growing Your Audience

You've published your podcast. Now comes the hard part: getting people to listen.

Let's set expectations honestly. Most podcasts grow slowly at first. The overnight success stories you've heard typically involve either existing audiences, significant marketing budgets, or years of work compressed into a triumphant narrative. Your path will probably look more humble.

## The Compounding Nature of Podcast Growth

Podcast audiences grow through compounding effects. Early on, you're building from zero—every listener is hard-won. But each listener who stays might tell one friend. Each guest you interview might share with their audience. Each appearance on another show might convert a few new subscribers.

These effects are small individually but compound over time. A show growing at 5% monthly doesn't feel like much in month three, but it's transformative over two years.

Your job is to create consistent value, show up reliably, and give compounding effects time to work.

## Organic Growth Strategies

**Create remarkable content:** This sounds obvious but bears repeating. The best marketing is content so valuable that listeners share it spontaneously. One exceptional episode that gets forwarded generates more growth than a month of promotional tactics.

**Optimize for discoverability:** Include relevant keywords in your episode titles and descriptions. When someone searches for topics you cover, your show should appear. Think about what your ideal listener would search for.

**Publish consistently:** Apps reward shows that publish reliably. Listeners form habits around consistent shows. Pick a schedule and stick to it, even when motivation wanes.

**Encourage reviews:** Apple Podcasts reviews influence rankings. Ask listeners to review at the end of episodes. Make it easy by linking directly to your Apple Podcasts page.

## Cross-Promotion and Guest Strategy

**Be a guest on other shows:** This is one of the most effective growth tactics. You access established audiences, demonstrate your value, and give listeners an easy path to find your show. Actively pitch yourself to relevant podcasts.

**Host guests with audiences:** When guests promote their episode to their followers, some percentage will become your listeners. Choose guests who will actually share, not just record.

**Cross-promote with similar shows:** Find shows with similar audiences and complementary (not competing) content. Trade promotions—they mention you, you mention them.

## Social Media Without Burning Out

Social media can support podcast growth but shouldn't consume your life.

**Repurpose, don't recreate:** Turn episode content into social posts. Quote graphics, audiogram clips, key takeaways. One episode can become ten pieces of social content.

**Focus on 1-2 platforms:** You don't need to be everywhere. Be excellent on the platforms where your audience actually spends time.

**Engage more than broadcast:** Commenting on others' posts, responding to mentions, and participating in communities builds relationships that passive posting never will.

**Batch your social work:** Designate specific times for social media rather than constant monitoring. Create a week's content in one session.

## Building an Email List

Email is your owned audience—not dependent on algorithms or platform changes. Build an email list from day one.

**Offer something valuable:** A checklist, resource guide, or bonus content in exchange for email signup. Not just "sign up for updates."

**Mention it in episodes:** The call-to-action most listeners can actually follow is "visit [website] to get [thing]."

**Email consistently:** Once you have subscribers, email them. Share new episodes, behind-the-scenes content, exclusive insights. An email list you never email is worthless.

## Podcast SEO

Search engine optimization applies to podcasts:

**Episode titles:** Include searchable keywords. "Interview with John Smith" is less searchable than "Building a $10M Business with John Smith."

**Show notes:** Detailed show notes with keywords help episodes rank in search engines and podcast app searches.

**Transcripts:** Full text transcripts make your content indexable. They're also great for accessibility.

**Your website:** A podcast page with properly structured content can rank for relevant searches and direct traffic to your show.

## Measuring Growth Realistically

**Download numbers:** The core metric. But understand that industry benchmarks vary wildly. A show with 1,000 downloads per episode is in the top 20% of podcasts.

**Growth rate:** More important than absolute numbers. Are downloads increasing month over month? Even modest consistent growth compounds significantly.

**Engagement:** Reviews, emails from listeners, social mentions. Small audience but high engagement often matters more than large numbers with no connection.

**Opportunities:** Speaking invitations, collaboration requests, business inquiries. These often matter more than vanity metrics.

## The Growth Mindset

Stop comparing your episode 20 to someone else's episode 300. Stop checking downloads daily. Stop assuming successful podcasts had advantages you don't.

Focus on creating the best possible episode this week. Then do it again next week. Incremental improvement over time beats sporadic brilliance.

Celebrate small wins: your first review, your first listener email, your first 100 downloads. These matter more than you think.

Your audience will grow if you deserve an audience. That sounds harsh but it's liberating. You can control whether you deserve an audience by consistently creating valuable content. You can't control when growth happens, but you can control whether you're worthy of it when it does.

Focus on what you control. The rest takes care of itself.
