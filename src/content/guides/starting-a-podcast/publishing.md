# Publishing and Distribution

You've recorded, edited, and branded your podcast. Now it needs to reach listeners. Distribution in 2026 is simpler than podcasting's early days, but strategic decisions matter more than ever.

## Choosing a Podcast Host

Your podcast hosting platform stores your audio files and generates the RSS feed that distributes to podcast apps. This is different from website hosting.

**What makes a good host:**
- Reliable uptime and fast downloads globally
- Unlimited bandwidth (or very high limits)
- Detailed analytics
- Easy RSS feed management
- Distribution to major directories
- Reasonable pricing that scales

**Popular options in 2026:**

**Buzzsprout** ($12-24/month): Most beginner-friendly. Clean interface, helpful tutorials, basic website included, magic mastering feature. Perfect starter choice.

**Transistor** ($19-99/month): Professional features with unlimited uploads. Supports multiple shows on one account. Good for building a podcast network.

**Captivate** ($19-99/month): Strong analytics and growth tools. Built-in ad insertion. Good for serious monetization focus.

**Libsyn** ($5-80/month): One of the oldest hosts. Pricing based on storage, not downloads. Interface feels dated but rock-solid reliability.

Most hosts offer free trials. Test before committing.

## Understanding RSS Feeds

RSS (Really Simple Syndication) is the technology that powers podcast distribution. Your host generates an RSS feed URL—a single link that contains all your episode information.

When you submit this URL to Apple Podcasts, Spotify, and other directories, they periodically check it for new episodes and update their listings automatically.

You don't need to understand RSS technically. Just know:
- Your host creates and manages your RSS feed
- Directories pull from this feed automatically
- Changing your feed URL breaks your listings (avoid this)
- Your feed URL should be permanent and stable

## Submitting to Directories

Once your host generates your RSS feed, submit to directories:

**Apple Podcasts:** Still the largest directory. Submit through Apple Podcasts Connect. Approval takes a few days. This listing often automatically populates other apps that pull from Apple's database.

**Spotify:** Submit through Spotify for Podcasters. Quick approval, usually 24-48 hours.

**Amazon Music/Audible:** Growing platform, worth the submission.

**Google Podcasts:** Transitioning to YouTube, but still worth listing.

**Other directories:** Stitcher, Podcast Index, iHeartRadio, and dozens of others. Your host may have one-click submission to many of these.

Focus on Apple and Spotify first—they represent the vast majority of listeners. Add others over time.

## YouTube as a Podcast Platform

YouTube is now a significant podcast consumption platform. Options:

**Full video episodes:** If you record video, publish complete episodes. This requires additional production effort but reaches YouTube's massive audience.

**Audio with static image:** Upload your audio with your cover art as a static video. Minimal effort, captures YouTube searches.

**Clips and highlights:** Create 2-10 minute highlight clips from longer episodes. This drives discovery better than full episodes.

Many podcasters create audio-first content, then upload to YouTube with minimal video—either static images or simple webcam recordings. YouTube's algorithm can surface your content to people who'd never find you in podcast apps.

## Video Podcasting Considerations

Video adds complexity but offers benefits:

**Pros:**
- YouTube discoverability
- Clips for social media
- Viewers feel more connected seeing faces
- Opens additional revenue streams

**Cons:**
- More equipment (camera, lighting)
- Longer editing time
- Harder to record remotely with quality
- Some content works better as audio-only

If you're starting fresh, master audio first. Add video once your audio production is solid and you're confident in your workflow. Don't let video ambitions delay your launch.

## The Launch Checklist

Before publishing your first episode:

- ✓ Hosting account set up with payment method
- ✓ Show metadata complete (title, description, categories, artwork)
- ✓ First 3-5 episodes uploaded and scheduled
- ✓ RSS feed tested and validated
- ✓ Submitted to Apple, Spotify, and other directories
- ✓ Website or landing page live with subscribe links
- ✓ Social media accounts created
- ✓ Email signup ready (if applicable)

## Launch Strategy

Don't launch with just one episode. Launch with 3-5 episodes so new listeners can binge and form a habit immediately.

Coordinate your launch day:
- Release episodes on your host
- Share across all social platforms
- Email your existing network
- Ask friends and colleagues to subscribe and review
- Post in relevant communities where you're already a member

First-week momentum matters for discoverability algorithms. Front-load your promotion effort.

## Managing Multiple Platforms

Once your podcast exists everywhere, maintain consistency:

**Audio stays the same:** Don't create platform-exclusive content that fragments your audience unless you have a specific strategy.

**Optimize per platform:** Apple values reviews and subscriptions. Spotify has video features and interactive tools. YouTube wants watch time. Learn what each platform rewards.

**Check analytics across platforms:** Your host shows total downloads; individual platforms show their specific metrics. Get the full picture.

Distribution is largely a one-time setup. Once systems are in place, focus shifts entirely to content creation and audience growth.
