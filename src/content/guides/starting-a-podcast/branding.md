# Cover Art and Branding

Your podcast's visual identity shapes first impressions. In a sea of search results, your cover art is often the deciding factor in whether someone clicks or scrolls past.

## Designing Podcast Artwork That Works

Podcast cover art appears as a small square—often just 150x150 pixels in podcast apps. Design for that reality.

**Simplicity wins:** Complex designs become muddy blobs at small sizes. One clear image or icon, your podcast name, and maybe a tagline. That's it.

**Text must be readable:** If your podcast name isn't legible at thumbnail size, simplify it. Large, bold fonts. Minimal words. Test by viewing your design at actual thumbnail size before finalizing.

**Color creates contrast:** Bold colors stand out in app feeds. Avoid muddy browns and grays unless they're intentional brand choices. Your artwork should pop against both light and dark app backgrounds.

**Faces often work:** If you're the personality behind the show, your face on the cover creates personal connection. If you're interviewing famous guests, their faces might rotate on episode-specific artwork.

**Technical specs:** 3000x3000 pixels, JPEG or PNG, RGB color. These specs work for all major platforms.

If design isn't your strength, tools like Canva have podcast cover templates. Or hire a designer on Fiverr or 99designs—a good podcast cover costs $50-200 and lasts for years.

## Creating a Cohesive Brand Identity

Your podcast brand extends beyond cover art:

**Color palette:** 2-3 colors that appear consistently in your cover art, website, social media, and episode graphics.

**Typography:** 1-2 fonts used consistently. One for headlines, one for body text.

**Voice and tone:** How you communicate in descriptions, social media, and episode titles. Professional or casual? Serious or playful?

Consistency builds recognition. When someone sees your episode graphic on social media, they should immediately recognize it as yours.

## Writing Compelling Episode Titles

Titles appear in podcast apps and search results. They're doing marketing work whether you design them to or not.

**Clarity over cleverness:** "How to Start Investing with $100" beats "Money Moves: Getting Started." People search for practical things, not clever wordplay.

**Include keywords:** If your episode covers "email marketing," those words should appear in the title. This helps with search discoverability.

**Create curiosity:** "The $50 Million Mistake That Saved My Business" makes you want to know the story. Specificity creates intrigue.

**Keep it scannable:** Long titles get truncated in most podcast apps. Front-load the interesting part.

## Visual Assets for Social Media

Each episode needs promotional graphics. At minimum:
- Square graphic for Instagram/Facebook
- Horizontal graphic for Twitter/LinkedIn
- Vertical graphic for Instagram Stories

Create a template once, then swap text and images for each episode. Canva's batch creation features help with this.

**Audiograms**—short video clips of your audio with waveform visualization—can increase engagement on social media. Tools like Headliner and Descript create these easily.

## Your Podcast Website

You need a home base on the web. Options:

**Your podcast host's built-in site:** Most hosts (Buzzsprout, Transistor, etc.) include a basic website. It's simple but functional.

**Your existing website:** Add a podcast section to your business or personal site. This consolidates your online presence.

**A dedicated podcast site:** Tools like Podpage ($12/month) create professional podcast websites with episode players, guest pages, and newsletter signup.

At minimum, your site needs:
- Episodes with embedded players
- Subscribe links to all platforms
- About page with host bio
- Contact information
- Email signup

## Brand Guidelines Document

As your show grows, document your brand choices:
- Logo files in various formats
- Color codes (hex values)
- Font names
- Voice and tone guidelines
- Example copy

This becomes essential if you ever hire help or collaborate with others.

## The Branding Truth

Great content with mediocre branding outperforms mediocre content with great branding every time.

Spend enough effort on branding that you look professional and trustworthy. Don't agonize over color choices for three weeks. Make reasonable decisions, execute consistently, and focus your energy on creating great episodes.

Your branding can always evolve later. Some of the most successful podcasts started with artwork that makes them cringe now. They succeeded because the content was valuable.

Create something you're not embarrassed by, then focus relentlessly on making episodes people want to share.
