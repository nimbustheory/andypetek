I've written countless articles and multiple books over the years that I never released. For some reason or another I always decided it wasn't the time on my journey. There was more knowledge I needed to gain or more professional clout. Between self-criticism and perfectionism, some of my greatest tools to share remained on the shelf in notebooks. Over all the years I've been working with small businesses there are several common themes. Fundamentals are often skipped and some of that "Business School 101" stuff falls through the cracks in the school of practical knowledge. There are books out there for "startup to stardom" and procedural tools for starting a new business, but many entrepreneurs and business owners who have already been around for a while assume they won't gain value. They assume these are textbook-style volumes of dry information and dictionary definitions that aren't applicable on the streets.

One through line of my career has been storytelling. Often visual storytelling as a designer and photographer, but also experiential implementation in event production, and actual storytelling through media. Part of the reason I didn't want to publish my books was I didn't think I was in the right chapter of my story. That's when I realized it's ALL about the story. A business is a story just like any career…or life. It doesn't happen in linear stages or need to be followed in a pre-prescribed sequence. It meanders and bounces forward and backwards based on who is in the driver seat.

So I decided to write a book about it. The toolbox for the journey in a story model that is more engaging to read than a textbook. And this is my pledge to set the date. By the end of this article, I will announce when this book will be available.

## Introducing Business Quest

Every great story follows the same arc. A hero answers a call to adventure, faces trials and challenges, gains allies and mentors, confronts their greatest fears, and emerges transformed. Joseph Campbell called it the monomyth—the Hero's Journey—and it's the narrative backbone of everything from Star Wars to The Lord of the Rings.

What struck me during fifteen years of building businesses is how perfectly this ancient storytelling structure maps onto the entrepreneurial experience. Starting a business isn't just a business decision—it's a quest. You leave the safety of the ordinary world (your day job), cross thresholds into unknown territory, face challenges that test everything you thought you knew, and emerge on the other side fundamentally changed.

*Business Quest: Your Hero's Journey from Idea to Empire* takes this framework and runs with it, using gaming language and quest metaphors to make entrepreneurship accessible, engaging, and—dare I say—fun.

## The Structure

The book is structured in four parts mirroring the stages of both the Hero's Journey and a classic video game campaign:

**Part I: Character Creation** covers the foundation—discovering your business idea, validating your concept, and setting up your legal structure. Just like rolling your stats before starting an RPG, entrepreneurs need to assess their strengths, choose their "class" of business, and select their "starting gear" before entering the arena.

**Part II: Early Game** tackles the building phase—funding your venture ("gathering resources"), assembling your team ("recruiting your party"), and developing your product ("crafting your first weapon"). These early chapters mirror that vulnerable stage in any game where you're still learning the mechanics and building your capabilities.

**Part III: Mid Game** addresses growth and operations—marketing and customer acquisition ("expanding your territory"), sales and revenue generation ("leveling up"), and building operational systems ("unlocking new abilities"). This is where the grind happens, where daily execution determines whether you advance or stall.

**Part IV: End Game** covers scaling and sustainability—organizational leadership at scale ("guild leadership"), managing the inevitable crises ("boss battles"), and planning your exit or legacy ("legacy quests"). The book even addresses serial entrepreneurship as "New Game+"—that moment when you've beaten the final boss and decide to start the adventure all over again with everything you've learned.

## Real Stories, Quest Treatment

Throughout, real business stories get the quest treatment. Steve Jobs' battle with IBM becomes a "boss fight." Netflix's disruption of Blockbuster unfolds as a "stealth mode" campaign. Tesla's progression from batteries to cars to energy to solar illustrates a "tech tree" strategy.

Each chapter includes practical worksheets framed as "Quest Completion" exercises, turning abstract business concepts into actionable steps you can take this week.
